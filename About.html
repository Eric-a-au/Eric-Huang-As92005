How has Climate Change affect us?

What does climate change do to New Zealand?

Floods, storms, droughts and fires will become more frequent unless significant action is taken to reduce global emissions of greenhouse gases, which are changing the climate.
1.coastal margins
2.flooding from rivers
3.availability of and competition for freshwater

5.threats to unique ecosystems
6.flow‑on effects from climate change impacts and responses overseas.

Cause of climate change

Human activities drive global warming trend since mid-20th century. One of the main cause of climate change is due to the increase greenhouse gases. Greenhouse effect is essential to life on earth because of human emissions that are trapped heat and slowing heat loss from the atmosphere. Five key greenhouse gases are carbon dioxide,nitrous oxide,methane, water vapor, and chlorofluorocarbons.

Possible climate change impact on New Zealand

rising temperatures, changing rainfall, andmore extreme weather events can effect new zealand ecosystems, water resources, agriculture, coastal areas, and communities. Key risks include sea level rise, increase drought and flooding, biodiversity loss, and threats to Māori cultural sites. Adaptation and emissions reduction are essential to manage these impacts.

New Zealand’s Climate Change Actions, and kaitiakitanga of cultural sites

New Zealand’s guiding principles of Kaitiakitanga, guardianship, strongly connect with the theme of the conference. We know that Climate Change is already affecting our cities. In New Zealand, our sea level has risen by an average of 1.81 millilitres per year since we began keeping records. Seal level rises make coastal communities and infrastructure vulnerable to floods, tsunamis, and other natural disasters. In other areas of the moth, particularly the east coast, increasingly warmer and drier climates face increased fire risk, and are threatened if they rely on rainwater.

Kaitiakitanga

guardianship and protection, refers to the responsibility to care for and protect the land, water, and other natural resources for future generations.
